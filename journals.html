The Hindustan Review

The journal is intended to serve as a forum for discussion and exposition of the various aspects of Indian and Buddhist learnings, and their applicability in societies of the world.

It does not seek to be a vehicle for political or religious commentary. The aim of the Journal is to remain objective, scholarly, and informative.

Contributions are invited from scholars across all walks of life interested in social, political, administrative, religious, cultural, peace, literature, and related fields.

Contributors are requested to send signed copies of their contributions in two sets (hard copy) and by email (soft copy). Publishers desiring review of their publications are requested to send two copies of their publications.
